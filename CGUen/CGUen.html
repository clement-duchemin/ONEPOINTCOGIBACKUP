The Cotigum Platform was created as part of the scientific research conducted by Nina FRANIATTE and Wim DE NEYS at the Laboratory of Child Development and Education Psychology (LaPsyDÉ), UMR CNRS 8240, Université Paris Cité. The main goal of this study is to understand whether video explanations can help improve reasoning abilities.

2 - STUDY CONTEXT

Since the second half of the 20th century, research in cognitive sciences has highlighted human reasoning's vulnerability to cognitive biases and certain logical errors and reasoning failures. Dual-process theory explains this biased thinking by describing human reasoning as an interaction between two types of processes or "systems": A fast and intuitive system (often called "System 1") and a slower, more deliberate one (often called "System 2"). Traditionally, "System 1" is identified as the source of the biased response, while "System 2" is seen as correcting the error to achieve logical reasoning.

However, recent debiasing studies show that a brief explanation - in text form - of the correct solution to a reasoning problem improves the performance of a majority of initially biased individuals, from the intuitive stage onward. In other words, we can learn to use our "System 1" to arrive at a correct answer quickly, at a low cognitive cost, and without necessarily having to rely on our "System 2" to correct an initially biased response.

While these textual explanations work for a majority of reasoners, there remains a significant number of subjects who are biased even after reading them (20% on average). Our research team has thus taken an interest in these biased reasoners.

This project aims to make these video explanations available to the general public through the implementation of a platform named Cogitum. It will offer three types of problems (games) from reasoning tasks commonly used in the literature on biases.
